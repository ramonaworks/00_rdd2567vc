Problem Discovery

Beginner developers struggled with fragmented documentation, overly technical explanations, and unclear learning paths. Existing resources assumed prior knowledge and lacked structured progression. The opportunity was to design a beginner-first learning system that reduced cognitive overload and accelerated time to first successful build.

Product strategy

Instead of building an LMS upfront, demand was validated through educational YouTube content. Organic reach confirmed market pull. From there, I designed a modular curriculum, built email capture infrastructure, developed supporting resources, and launched a paid product. Each step reduced risk while increasing signal.

“Validate before you scale.
Structure before you sell.”

— Product principle

Lifecycle Components

  • Organic top-of-funnel acquisition via YouTube
  • Email capture and owned audience growth
  • Curriculum architecture and modular course design
  • Monetized digital product launch

Acquisition and growth

Built a trust-based funnel from content to subscription to purchase. YouTube served as demand validation and awareness. Email became the owned distribution channel. Educational resources increased perceived value before conversion. The system reduced dependency on paid traffic and created compounding audience growth.

Product architecture

Designed a step-by-step curriculum with clear progression, minimizing overwhelm and increasing completion likelihood. Focused on clarity, modularity, and beginner-first UX. Structured the product for self-paced learning and real-world application rather than passive consumption.

Monetization model

Launched a paid digital product after validating audience intent. Conversion occurred after trust stacking through free educational value. This approach demonstrated full-cycle ownership, from discovery and validation to revenue generation.
